Dispersion of white light

☆ The phenomenon of separation of white light into seven colors when it passes through a glass prism is know as dispersion of light.


when white light is passed through a glass prism it is separated into distinct color components..This is known as light dispersion.

A white ray of light is divided into seven colors by a prism, ie; Violet,indigo,blue,green,yellow,orange,and red.

The band of colors scattered by light entering a glass prism is refered to as a spectrum.

So,what is a prism?

A prism is a transparent optical device with polished surfaces that refract light

Usually -A prism is refered to as a triangular object made of glass having two triangular bases and three rectangular sides inclined at a certain angle to refract light.

So, what exactly is Refraction of light?
This refers to the change in direction of the propagation of light as it passes through different medium.

What causes Dispersion of light?

Isn't a rainbow beautiful?,have you ever wondered how a rainbow is formed?
☁ It is actually due to a basic physics phenomenon known as dispersion of light.

✎ Dispersion of light is caused some of the following factors;


✽ When white light passes through a glass prism it's constituent colors,(red,orange, yellow,Green,blue,indigo,violet) travel with different velocities/speeds in the prism because refractive index is color dependant.This causes dispersion of light.

✽ It is also caused by the change of light rays (resulting into angle of deviation) of each wave length by a different amount.

Angle of deviation

is the angle equal to the difference between the angle of incident and the angle of refraction of a ray of light passing through the surface between one medium and another of different refractive index.

Rainbow

A rainbow is an example of white light dispersion. A high number of tiny droplets of water remain hanging in the atmosphere shortly after rain.

Each drop functions as a tiny prism when light strikes the droplets the white light divides into seven colors of the spectrum.

A continous band of seven colors is formed by the scattered light from a huge number of droplets.
